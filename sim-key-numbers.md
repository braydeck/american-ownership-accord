## Sim-5: Market Stabilization (AMCF Float Reduction Model)

Parameters: 15% AMCF passive market ownership (Phase 3, ~Year 13 per Sim-6). AMCF holds equity permanently — never sells, never margins, never redeems.

### Three Historical Crashes at 15% AMCF Passive Ownership

| Crash | Forced Sell % | Actual Depth | AMCF Simulated | Improvement | Actual Recovery | AMCF Recovery |
|-------|--------------|-------------|----------------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| 2008 Financial Crisis | 65% | 56.6% | ~45.9% | −10.7pp | 66 months | ~53 months |
| 2020 COVID Crash | 45% | 34.0% | ~28.9% | −5.1pp | 6 months | ~6 months |
| 2022 Bear Market | 40% | 25.5% | ~21.9% | −3.6pp | 24 months | ~22 months |

Model: Crash depth reduction = AMCF share × forced-sell fraction × 1.2 (second-order cascade feedback).
At 15% AMCF: reduction = 15% × 65% × 1.2 = 11.7% amplitude reduction → 2008 depth = 56.6% × (1 − 0.117) = 49.9%.

### AMCF Market Share by Phase (Sim-6 equity trajectory)

| Year | AMCF Equity | AMCF Own % (Sim-6) | Notes |
|------|-------------|---------------------|-------|
| 1    | $0.59T      | 1.1%                | Minimal stabilization effect |

| 15   | $27.15T     | 16.6%               | Phase 3: strong stabilization; 15% threshold crossed ~Year 12 |
| 20   | $51.87T     | 20% (cap)           | Maximum ownership cap reached |

At 15%+ AMCF market share (~Year 12+), the 2008-style crash would be cut from 57% to ~50% depth.
At 20% cap, the stabilizing effect is very large — effectively preventing cascading margin call dynamics.

## Appendix: Worker Equity Three-Tier Architecture

| Tier | Firm Threshold | Mechanism | Worker Receives |
|------|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Tier 1 | <$10M EV | $1,000/yr employer contribution to Sectoral Fund | Fund dividends (3.5% of balance); portable across jobs |
| Tier 2 | $10M–$100M EV | Phantom equity ($25K–$100K/worker); dividends routed to Sectoral Fund | Fund dividends + cashout at FMV on departure |
| Tier 3 | >$100M EV | 4%/yr Equity Excise → 20% PSU pool by Year 5 | PSU dividends (3.5% yield) + cashout at FMV on departure |

**Steady-state (Year 10+) annual income from worker equity by bracket:**
- $25–40K worker: ~$6,000/yr dividends + ~$16,000/yr annualized cashout
- $55–75K worker: ~$8,500/yr dividends + ~$22,000/yr annualized cashout
- $100–150K worker: ~$13,000/yr dividends + ~$35,000/yr annualized cashout

PSU values appreciate at 7.5%/yr nominal (EV growth) after the 5-year ramp.
Tier 1 Sectoral Fund is portable — no cashout event on job change.
